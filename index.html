Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Radio waves are used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) to provide an image of the inside of the body.

MRIs are often performed at UTMI to aid in the evaluation and diagnosis of various illnesses.

Interventional Radiology

A branch of this science called interventional radiology uses some of the same cutting-edge imaging technologies to treat patients as well as just taking pictures.

Radiology Services Offered

The UTMI radiology team performs the following diagnostic procedures:

  • Bone Density Scan

    Our non-invasive bone densitometer detects calcium levels in bones to assist diagnose osteoporosis risk in patients.

  • Nuclear Medicine

    Our doctors use nuclear medicine to assess organ function, find malignancies, and diagnose certain illnesses.

  • Mammography

    The mostcommon way to test for breast cancer is using mammography. It uses X-rays to assist in the detection of breast cancers or other abnormal growths.

  • Ultrasound

    Ultrasound, is the least intrusive imaging technique currently accessible. Our specialists use sonograms to peer inside the body to assess various organs and blood arteries

  • X-ray

    Electromagnetic waves are used in this diagnostic process to capture pictures of the body. Without having to make an incision, it allows your doctor to see inside your body.

  • Nuchal Translucency

    This screening test, which is carried out in the first trimester of a pregnancy in conjunction with ultrasound technology, can assess the risk of specific diseases like Down syndrome, chromosomal abnormalities, and cardiac issues.
